Welcome to our website
Exploring poverty in Hong Kong

Connecting with the poor

In order to truly connect with the homeless, it is essential that we are being directly involved with them. Without actively interacting and engaging in conversation with the homeless, we are unable to understand their wants and needs.

Spreading Kindness

Loneliness is a cruel reality for those that are homeless, an issue that most are unaware of. We aim to empower the homeless by providing them with kindness. We hope our actions and goodwill can remind them that they are cared for.

Alleviating Poverty

Although poverty and homelessness are serious problems, they cannot be dealt with in a day, month or year. However, we are still able to contribute our efforts to fight it, through donations, volunteering, and raising awareness.

ImpactHK

ImpactHK is an NGO that aims to assist the homeless by providing them with a sense of security, friendship, and kindness. They currently host 2 activities, Kindness Walks and The Guest Room.

The Kindness Walk is an activity where volunteers join together and head out to different areas of Hong Kong to distribute necessities to the homeless. Distributed items include bananas, wet wipes, soaps, hard-boiled eggs, and water bottles.



The Guest Room, located in Tai Kok Tsui, is the headquarters of ImpactHK, where the homeless or those in need are welcomed in at any time of the day. Inside, there are pool tables, cooking appliances, sofas, a projector, canned foods, and air conditioning. When we went there to conduct our interview with Jeff Rotmeyer, we saw first hand how effective The Guest Room was. There were homeless people doing all sorts of activities, from playing a game of pool with Jeff, to watching a movie in the lounge area. It is evident that the homeless share a strong friendship with the workers at ImpactHK, one which was built up through countless hours of counseling and activities.

FeedingHK

Feeding Hong Kong is a NGO that benefits the community by providing leftover food from restaurants and bakeries for the poor. Through their work, they are able to solve two problems, reduce wasted food going to landfills, as well as giving back to the underprivileged people living in Hong Kong.

FeedingHK is a B2B logistics hub. Which means that they collect surplus foodfrom food companies. After sorting and labelling the stocks, FeedingHK redistributes the food to other charities that provide food services to minorities.



The Existing Issues in Hong Kong regarding Poverty & The Homeless

Hong Kong is ranked the #1 most expensive city to live in in the world. According to https://www.worldatlas.com/articles/the-most-expensive-cities-in-the-world-for-real-estate.html (Khushboo Sheth )Hong Kong is the second most expensive city to buy housing (first being Monaco, Europe), with an average of 20 square meters of property per USD $1 million. The reason for Hong Kong’s exorbitant housing prices is mostly due to supply-and-demand. Given Hong Kong’s has a (relatively) small land mass, land and property costs more than cities with an excess of property and developable land.

What can we do to help?

According to this Vox's Episode:

A cause for Hong Kong's Housing Crisis is the city’s poor land management. The government of Hong Kong owns all of the land in Hong Kong, and leases them out in an auction process, giving the contract to the highest bidder. Given the scarcity of these contracts, more and more mainland Chinese real estate agencies pour money into these small plots of land, and results in an extremely high price. A lot of the government’s revenue relies on land sales, around 30% actually.

Because of their highly profitable land business, Hong Kong has astronomically low taxes, which is the main cause of their economic freedom in this country. However, the land leased to the agencies in high prices have to become profit, causing the prices to rise rapidly.
